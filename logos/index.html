From the sketchbook · من الكرّاس

The mark was built the way old marks were — pencil first, then ink, then the name written into it. Each step left a small imperfection on purpose. Symmetry is for printers; humans aren't.

01 · pencil
→
A rough teardrop, drawn quickly. Left side fuller — like a real cloth bag sagging with dates.
02 · tie it
→
Cord rising from the gather, tied in a small knot. Off-vertical, the way a hand pulls it.
03 · ink it
→
Two cloth folds, weighted unequally. Two tiny stitch dots at the neck. Nothing perfect.
04 · let it be
No accent. No flourish. The pouch alone says enough — that's the whole brand promise.

The same pouch resolves differently depending on what you bring to it. Sustenance, recognition, measure — three faces of the same drawing, three pieces of what the app does.

Why this mark, for this app

Zad photographs a meal, recognises the dish, estimates the grams, and tracks it across the day. The mark says all of that without a single word: a cloth pouch (food packed with care), an eye (recognition), a vessel (measure). No ornament. The pouch alone is the whole product — care, carried, in five quiet lines.

— الزادُ ما يُكفي، لا ما يُترَف —

Zad is what suffices — not what is excessive.
